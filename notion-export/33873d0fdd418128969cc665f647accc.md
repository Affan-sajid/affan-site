**Theme:** Saturation, maturity, and diminishing novelty

**Core question:** What does it mean when a technology finishes its arc?

**What this article is really about**

- The internet didn’t fail. It completed its job.
- Why everything feels “the same” now.
- Why new Googles and Facebooks are unlikely *within the same substrate*.
- How arcs end not with collapse, but with sameness.
**Key ideas**

- Maturity ≠ stagnation, but it kills novelty.
- Platforms optimize, not reinvent.
- Innovation shifts elsewhere when primitives are exhausted.
**This article does NOT**

- Talk about AI deeply
- Predict the future
- Propose solutions
It’s a **diagnosis**, not a prescription.
